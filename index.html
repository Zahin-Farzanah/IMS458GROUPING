History of Kampung Gelang Mas

Early Settlement


Kampung Gelang Mas was established in the early 19th century by Malay settlers who were attracted to the fertile lands and strategic location. The village name translates to "Golden Bracelet Village," reflecting the prosperity and beauty of the area.

Cultural Development


Over the decades, the village developed its unique cultural identity, blending traditional Malay customs with influences from various ethnic communities. This rich cultural tapestry is still evident today in local festivals, crafts, and daily life.

Traditional Crafts


The village became known for its skilled artisans who specialized in traditional crafts such as woodcarving, batik making, and silver jewelry. These crafts continue to be practiced and celebrated today.

Modern Preservation


Today, Kampung Gelang Mas balances modernization with heritage preservation, maintaining its traditional character while welcoming visitors from around the world to experience authentic Malaysian village life.

Historical Landmark

Masjid Kampung Laut was built in the 15th century by Champa traders. Its design reflects local and South Indian influences. Originally simple, it was expanded during the Kelantan Sultanate (1859–1900) and became a key meeting and trading place. In 1970, it was handed over to the Kelantan government. The mosque is located about 20 km from Kampung Gelang Mas, Pasir Mas. Kampung Laut has long been a traditional gathering spot.
Pasir Mas was founded by Che Leh Ismail, also known as Che Leh Tok Pendekar, after he moved from Kota Bharu when the Sultan took his land for a palace.
One day, after a religious teacher from Bachok finished teaching the Quran there, three pots of gold were said to appear and roll into a nearby pond. Villagers shouted “Mas! Mas!” (which means gold), leading to the name Pasir Mas. “Pasir” (sand) came from the large sandy area near the Kelantan River.

Shopping

Every Thursday, a morning market is held near Gelang Mas Mosque. Residents of Kampung Gelang Mas and nearby areas go to the market to shop for items such as vegetables, food, clothing, and other goods. The prices at the morning market are reasonable and affordable.
Pasar Bandar Tasek Raja in Pasir Mas, Kelantan is a popular and modern marketplace offering fresh produce, local food, clothes, and household items at affordable prices. With its attractive Islamic-style architecture, it serves as a convenient shopping spot for locals and visitors. Located about 4–5 km from Kampung Gelang Mas, it takes only 8–10 minutes to reach by car or motorcycle, making it an accessible and essential destination for daily needs.
The Perusahaan Serunding dan Dodol Wan Sabariah Pasir Mas, also known as Rumah Hijau due to its striking green and yellow traditional house, is a well-known local shopping destination in Pasir Mas, Kelantan. This family-run business specializes in **Kelantanese traditional delicacies such as serunding (spicy meat floss) and dodol (sweet toffee-like dessert). Visitors often stop here to buy these popular treats as souvenirs or gifts. The business operates daily from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM, and its vibrant and unique traditional house design also makes it a tourist attraction. It’s not just a food shop—it's an experience of local culture and flavors.

You Must Go

The image shows the magnificent Sleeping Buddha statue at Wat Phothivihan in Tumpat, Kelantan, one of the largest reclining Buddha statues in Southeast Asia and a must-visit landmark for anyone exploring the region. This impressive 40-meter-long statue lies serenely within the temple grounds, surrounded by peaceful pavilions and beautiful Buddhist architecture, making it an ideal destination for those interested in culture, spirituality, and photography. Wat Phothivihan is approximately 5 kilometers from Kampung Gelang Mas, Pasir Mas, with a drive taking about 3-5 minutes—perfect for a memorable half-day trip to experience Kelantan’s unique cultural heritage.
The Kathina Ceremony is a revered tradition in Theravāda Buddhism, held annually after the conclusion of the monastic rainy retreat known as Vassa. At Wat Phothivihan in Kampung Gelang Mas, Pasir Mas, Kelantan famously home to the giant Sleeping Buddha. The ceremony transforms the temple grounds into a vibrant spiritual gathering. During this event, devotees offer new robes and essential items to Buddhist monks as an act of generosity and merit-making. The atmosphere is filled with prayer chants, community meals, and cultural displays that blend Thai and Malay village influences. It's more than just a religious ritual, it is a moment of shared values, reflection, and celebration deeply rooted in the heart of Gelang Mas.

Community

Meet the heart of the village — the people. Discover how traditions, family values, and cooperation shape daily life.

Kg Gelang Mas in Pasir Mas, Kelantan is known for its strong dikir barat tradition.The village has produced famous performers like Hussein Gelang Mas, known for his witty and spontaneous verses.Dikir barat is a traditional group performance that involves singing, pantun (rhymed verses), and traditional instruments like the rebana.

Community in Kg Gelang Mas:

1. Melayu (Malay) The majority of the population in Kampung Gelang Mas consists of Malay Muslims. The community is closely-knit and practices strong Islamic and cultural traditions. Daily life revolves around the mosque, local markets, and family gatherings. Traditional practices like kenduri, and gotong-royong are still widely observed. Most villagers speak in the Kelantanese dialect, which is distinct and rich in local expressions.
2. Siam (Thai/Malay Siamese) Kampung Gelang Mas, like many other villages in Kelantan, is also home to a smaller population of Malay-Siam or Thai Muslims. They share a similar lifestyle with the Malays, but preserve unique customs such as traditional Thai-style house architecture, food practices, and cultural celebrations. Their presence contributes to the rich cultural blend in the village. They also speak Thai or Siamese dialects alongside Malay, and many are bilingual.

Event

Celebrate with the village! Learn about festivals, performances, and seasonal happenings that bring Kampung Gelang Mas to life.

In Kampung Gelang Mas, the spirit of togetherness truly shines during the holy month of Ramadan. As sunset approaches, villagers come together to prepare and distribute iftar meals, ensuring that no one in the community is left to break fast alone.
This tradition of food sharing often takes place at the mosque or community hall, where families volunteer ingredients, time, and effort to cook large quantities of dishes such as bubur lambuk, nasi briyani, or kurma ayam. Meals are then packed and distributed to nearby homes — especially to the elderly, single mothers, and those in need.
The practice is done with gotong-royong spirit, where everyone, from children to elders, plays a role — whether it's stirring the pot, packing the meals, or delivering them door to door. It's a beautiful example of community cooperation and compassion, turning every meal into an act of kindness and unity.

Every year, the village of Kampung Gelang Mas celebrates Maulidur Rasul with great spirit and devotion, reflecting the deep spiritual values and unity of the community. The celebration usually begins with a village-wide procession, joined by people from all walks of life — from the elderly to school children. Participants march together while chanting selawat (praises for the Prophet Muhammad PBUH), wearing coordinated outfits and carrying banners expressing love and respect for the Prophet.
The procession typically ends at the village surau or community hall, where a religious talk (ceramah agama) is held to remind everyone of the Prophet’s noble character and teachings. The event concludes with a communal feast, prepared through gotong-royong (communal effort), turning the religious occasion into a meaningful moment of togetherness and neighbourly harmony.

Another highlight in Kampung Gelang Mas is the traditional performance of Rebana Ubi — large, ornate drums played in unison by groups of villagers. In this village, rebana ubi is more than just a musical performance; it is a proud symbol of cultural identity and ancestral heritage, carefully preserved and passed down through generations.
The performance usually takes place at night, either at the community hall or open village field, often accompanied by silat (traditional martial arts) demonstrations and rhythmic drumming. It draws enthusiastic crowds, not only from the village but also from surrounding areas, reinforcing a strong sense of local pride and cultural continuity.
